# KPI Definitions

## EBITDA
Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation and Amortization.

Used to measure operational profitability excluding financial and accounting effects.

## Gross Profit
Total revenues minus cost of goods sold.

Indicates the profitability of core business activities.

## Net Financial Position (PFN)
Difference between financial debts and liquid assets.

Used to assess company financial stability and liquidity.

## Cash Flow
Net change in cash over a period.

Essential to monitor liquidity and short-term sustainability.

## Cost Drivers
Top cost categories impacting profitability.

Used to identify areas for optimization.

## Revenue Drivers
Top revenue sources.

Used to understand business performance and growth drivers.

## Net Debt Ratio (PFN / Equity)

This KPI measures the relationship between Net Financial Position and Equity, providing an indicator of the company’s financial leverage.

### Interpretation

| Value Range | Meaning |
|------------|--------|
| < 0 | Net cash position (no financial debt) – very strong financial structure |
| < 1 | Good financial balance – debt lower than equity |
| ≈ 1 | Balanced situation between debt and equity |
| > 1 | High financial leverage – debt exceeds equity |

### Business Insight

An increasing ratio indicates growing financial risk and dependence on external financing.

---

## Debt Sustainability Ratio (PFN / EBITDA)

This KPI measures how many years the company would theoretically need to repay its financial debt using its operating performance.

### Interpretation

| Value Range | Meaning |
|------------|--------|
| < 2 | Excellent sustainability – low financial risk |
| 2 – 3 | Acceptable – balanced situation |
| 3 – 4 | Warning – debt sustainability under pressure |
| > 4 | Risky – high financial exposure |

### Business Insight

This metric is widely used to assess the sustainability of financial debt relative to the company’s ability to generate cash flow.

Thresholds may vary depending on the industry:
- capital-intensive sectors → higher values can be acceptable
- tech / services → values above 2 may already indicate risk
